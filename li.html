Early Life & Career

A photo of Dr. Fei-Fei Li.
(Image source: Stanford HAI)

  • An Immigrant's Journey

    Born in Beijing, China, Fei-Fei Li moved to the United States with her parents at the age of 15. The family settled in New Jersey, where she faced many challenges, including a new language and culture.

  • Education at Princeton and Caltech

    She earned a scholarship to Princeton University, where she studied physics. She later earned her Ph.D. in electrical engineering from the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), where her interest in computer vision and AI began to blossom.

Major Contributions to AI

  • The ImageNet Project

    Dr. Li's most significant contribution is the creation of ImageNet. At a time when AI researchers were focused on algorithms, she believed that large-scale, high-quality data was the key to unlocking AI's potential.

  • Catalyzing the Deep Learning Revolution

    ImageNet, a massive database of over 14 million labeled images, provided the data needed to train powerful deep neural networks. The annual ImageNet Challenge became a benchmark for computer vision, accelerating the deep learning revolution and leading to breakthroughs in fields like self-driving cars and medical imaging.

A Lasting Influence

An image representing the concept of Human-Centered AI.

  • Human-Centered AI

    Dr. Li is a leading advocate for "human-centered AI," an approach that prioritizes developing AI systems that augment, rather than replace, human capabilities. She believes AI should be a tool for good that enhances human life and dignity.

  • The Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI)

    She co-founded the Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI) to lead the research, education, policy, and practice of this field. Her work highlights the importance of ethical considerations, diversity, and public education in the development of AI.
