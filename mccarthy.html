John McCarthy

A Pioneer of Artificial Intelligence

Explore the life and contributions of the man who coined the term "Artificial Intelligence."

Early Life & Education

Born in 1927, McCarthy was a child prodigy with a passion for math.

  • Graduated high school two years early.
  • Taught himself college-level math.
  • Earned his B.S. in Mathematics from **Caltech** in 1948.
  • Received a Ph.D. from **Princeton University** in 1951.

Coining "Artificial Intelligence"

In 1955, McCarthy proposed a summer workshop that would define a new field of study.

  • Proposed the **Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence**.
  • The workshop, held in 1956, is considered the **birthplace of AI** as an academic field.
  • Defined AI as: "The science and engineering of making intelligent machines."

Key Contributions

🧠

Symbolic AI

Focused on using formal logic to enable machines to reason and solve problems.

💻

Lisp Language

Created the first major programming language for symbolic computation.

☁️

Time-Sharing

Pioneered a method for multiple users to share a single computer's resources.

Enduring Legacy

McCarthy's contributions continue to influence modern AI and computing.

  • His vision of **symbolic AI** is still used in expert systems and logical reasoning.
  • Lisp's concepts of **functional programming** and recursion are found in popular modern languages like Python.
  • The AI labs he founded at MIT and Stanford trained generations of AI researchers.
