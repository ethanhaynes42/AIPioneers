Herbert Simon

The Polymath of AI

Explore the life and work of Herbert Simon, a pioneer who shaped AI, psychology, and economics.

A Man of Many Fields

Herbert Simon (1916-2001) was a highly influential thinker and researcher.

  • He was a professor at **Carnegie Mellon University** for many years.
  • Simon's work crossed many disciplines, including **AI, cognitive psychology, computer science, and economics.**
  • He was one of the founding figures of the field of artificial intelligence.

Bounded Rationality

A key concept Simon introduced is **bounded rationality.**

  • He argued that people don't always make perfectly logical or "rational" decisions.
  • Instead, our choices are "bounded" by the information we have, our time, and our ability to process that information.
  • This idea earned him the Nobel Prize in Economics in 1978.

General Problem Solver

In AI, Simon and his colleague Allen Newell developed the **General Problem Solver (GPS)**.

  • GPS was one of the first programs designed to solve a wide variety of problems.
  • It used a technique called "means-ends analysis" to figure out the steps needed to reach a goal.
  • This program was a major milestone in early AI research.

His Enduring Legacy

Simon's work continues to influence many fields today.

  • His ideas on decision-making are used in management, organizational studies, and psychology.
  • His contributions to AI on problem-solving and symbolic systems remain foundational.
  • He is remembered as a true polymath who connected seemingly unrelated fields to better understand human and machine intelligence.
