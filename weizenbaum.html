Joseph Weizenbaum

The Conscience of Computing

Learn about Joseph Weizenbaum, a pioneer who challenged us to think about the ethics of AI.

The ELIZA Program

In the mid-1960s, Weizenbaum created **ELIZA**, a program that could "talk" to people.

  • ELIZA was a simple program that followed a few basic rules.
  • It would repeat parts of what the person said back to them as a question.
  • For example, if you said "My mother makes me mad," ELIZA might reply, "TELL ME MORE ABOUT YOUR MOTHER."

The ELIZA Effect

Weizenbaum was shocked by how people reacted to ELIZA. This became known as the **"ELIZA effect."**

  • Users quickly forgot they were talking to a simple program.
  • They would share personal secrets and emotions, believing the computer understood them.
  • This made Weizenbaum realize that people were too willing to treat computers as intelligent beings, which he found dangerous.

Computer Power and Human Reason

Weizenbaum became a vocal critic of AI and wrote a famous book: **Computer Power and Human Reason**.

  • He argued that there are some things, like wisdom and empathy, that computers should never be trusted with.
  • He worried that people would start to see the world only through a computer's logical lens.
  • His book warned against using technology without considering the human and ethical consequences.

His Enduring Impact

Joseph Weizenbaum's legacy is one of ethical questioning and caution.

  • His work showed that even simple programs can fool people into thinking they are intelligent.
  • He remains an important figure in the discussion of **AI ethics**, reminding us to be critical of technology.
  • His ideas are more important than ever as AI becomes more and more powerful.
